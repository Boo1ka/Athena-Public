# Protocol 91: The Pearson Synthesis

> **Source**: Taylor Pearson's essay archive (2012-2023) + "The Ultimate Guide to Apprenticeships" (2018)
> **Purpose**: Formalize the meta-framework for transitioning from credential-based to outcome-based career strategy.

---

## 1. The Master Thesis

> **"The End of Jobs"** = The shift from permission-based work (credentials, hierarchy, tenure) to outcome-based work (leverage, shipping, portfolio).

| Old Model (Jobs) | New Model (Entrepreneurship) |
|------------------|------------------------------|
| Get degree → Get hired → Get paid | Build proof → Ship outcomes → Get paid |
| Permission-seeking | Value-generating |
| Linear career (40 years, one track) | Stair-stepping (many small bets, compounding) |
| Security = Tenure | Security = Optionality |

---

## 2. Key Frameworks Extracted

### 2.1 The Stair-Stepping Model

> "Start in very small, non-competitive markets. Stack wins. Move up."

| Step | Example (Rob Walling) | Principle |
|------|----------------------|-----------|
| 0 | Duck blind e-book ($500/mo) | Non-competitive niche, proof of concept |
| 1 | Electrician job board (local) | Stack a second income stream |
| 2 | Wedding website service | Diversify, learn new skills |
| 3 | DotNetInvoice (SaaS) | Bigger market, recurring revenue |
| 4 | Email marketing software | Scale, leverage, exit optionality |

**Application**: Start where competition is low. Don't fight for competitive markets until you have proof.

---

### 2.2 The 80/20 vs. 10,000 Hour Split

> "80% proficiency in 6-12 months. 99% proficiency in 10,000 hours."

| When to 80/20 | When to 10,000 Hour |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Skills that are **not** your core differentiator | Skills that **are** your core differentiator |
| Mitigating weakness to "competent" | Maximizing strength to "world-class" |
| Sales, finance, grammar | Systems architecture, contextualization |

**Heuristic**: "What do people ask you about that seems easy to you but impressive to them?" → 10,000 hour that.

---

### 2.3 Skills Are Multiplicative, Not Additive

> `Skill 1 × Skill 2 × Skill 3 = Ability`

**If any skill = 0, the whole thing = 0.**

| Every field has ~5 fundamentals. | You need basic competence in all, mastery in 1-2. |
|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Business: Sales, Innovation, Operations, Finance, Management | 80/20 four, 10,000 hour one |

**Application**: Audit your skill stack. Find the zeros. Fix them first.

---

### 2.4 Strengths ≠ Skills (The Hidden Layer)

| Skills (External) | Strengths (Internal) |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Marketing, Writing, Operations | **Contextualization** (Taylor's) |
| Tutoring, Trading, AI Dev | **Systems Architecture** (User's) |

**Heuristic**: "The success I've had with [Skill] is more a result of [Strength]."

**Application**: Identify the internal strength that runs through all your external skills. That's what you're really 10,000-houring.

---

### 2.5 Ship Constantly (The Ira Glass Protocol)

> "Put yourself on a deadline so that every week you will finish one story."

| Old Model | New Model |
|-----------|-----------|
| Perfect it, then ship | Ship it, then improve |
| Wait for permission | Create your own portfolio |
| Hide your work until "ready" | "Blog the book" (30-50% appears first) |

**Application**: The only way to discover your internal strength is to ship work and look for the thread that runs through it.
